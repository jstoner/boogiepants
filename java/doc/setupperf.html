setting up boogiepants for a performance

To use boogiepants in a performance, you need a few things:

  • a stage with a blank wall or screen suitable for projection;
  • a computer to run boogiepants on--a laptop works well. It needs to support bluetooth, and reasonably fast graphics;
  • actual pants--the garment that holds the motion sensors on your body;
  • a wiimote and nunchuk;
  • a projector to show the boogiepants display to the audience;
  • a display you can use to see boogiepants during your performance (assuming you will face the audience, and the projected display will appear behind you, you will need to have a display in front of you. Or you could rehearse a lot.) Again, a laptop works well, something like a teleprompter or heads-up display might be ideal. We would need to experiment with that;
  • something to put the display on, like a stool.

One feature useful for performance is full screen mode. Select View > Full Screen to go into full screen mode, and use pretzel-F to toggle back and forth.

It's also useful to set up your operating system to display the same image on both screens. On the Mac:

  1. Connect your projector;
  2. go into System Preferences > Displays. Click Detect Displays if necessary;
