design considerations for the garment

You can also design your own boogiepants garment. Bear in mind, boogiepants is just a silly name. The garment doesn't necessarily need to qualify as pants. It just needs to do these things:

  • Locate the nunchuk somewhere on the pelvis. The front of the hip seems most responsive so far, but further experimentation may be needed;
  • Locate the wii remote somewhere on the torso, probably above the belly. This allows you to move the two independently. Shorter dancers may need to locate it higher up on their torsos.
  • Allow the dancer to access the buttons on the wiimote during performance. You can put it in a pocket with strategically placed holes, or make it out of tight, sheer fabric. Just don't make it so tight the buttons get pushed accidentally.
  • Keep the sensors in place during a performance. We want to measure the motion of the dancer's body, not the flopping of the dancer's clothing. The garment will need to be form-fitting. At the same time, we don't want the garment to slide around too much.
  • Allow the wii remote and nunchuk to be removed and replaced easily. Batteries need to be replaced, and the garment may need to be washed at some point. Don't put your wiimote in the wash.
  • It's important that the wiimote go into its pocket from the left, and the buttons point out from the torso, both for interaction with the software and ease of interaction for the dancer.
  • It's important that the nunchuk go into its pocket from the top (pointing down), and that the joystick points towards the front of the body.
  • All boogiepants garments should be stylish and fun.
